The First Dream of King Pasenadi

2024-10-15T21:33:21.009Z

Original: https://discourse.suttacentral.net/t/the-first-dream-of-king-pasenadi/36389

In Ja 77, we find a list of sixteen items, which the commentary explains as summarizing sixteen dreams of King Pasenadi. The Buddha tells the king that these are portents of times to come.

I couldn’t help but be moved by the first of these dreams.

Hereafter, when kings shall be stingy and unrighteous, and when folk shall be unrighteous, in days when the world is perverted, when good is waning and evil waxing apace – in those days of the world’s discontent there shall fall no rain from the heavens, the feet of the storm shall be lamed, the crops shall wither, and famine shall be on the land. Then shall the clouds gather as if for rain from the four quarters of the heavens; there shall be haste first to carry indoors the rice and crops that the women have spread in the sun to dry, for fear the harvest should get wet; and then with spade and basket in hand the men shall go forth to bank up the dykes. As though in sign of coming rain, the thunder shall bellow, the lightning shall flash from the clouds – but even as the bulls in your dream that fought not, so the clouds shall flee away without raining.
